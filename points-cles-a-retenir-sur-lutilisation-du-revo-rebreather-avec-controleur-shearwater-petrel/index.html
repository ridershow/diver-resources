Key Points to Remember on Using the rEvo Rebreather with Shearwater Petrel Controller

Par Jérémy Lanfranchi
14/05/2025

Vital Safety Information

  1. Oxygen Cells - The cornerstone of your safety:
    • They are galvanic cells that measure O₂ concentration
    • Replacement strategy: change one cell every 6 months (not all at once)
    • Check current limitation during O₂ flushing at 6m (cells should not be limited before 3-4 bar)
  2. PpO₂ Monitoring - Constant vigilance:
    • Mandatory check every minute
    • Compare values between Shearwater and Revodream (cross-checking)
    • Anticipate depth variations that affect PpO₂
  3. Setpoint Management:
    • Surface: always low SP
    • Descent: switch to high SP at 20m (slow descent) or at maximum depth (square profile)
    • Ascent: switch to low SP at 15m (no-deco dive) or at the end of the last stop (deco dive)
  4. Verification Procedures:
    • Build check, Close check, Prejump check before entering water
    • The "3Bs" in water: Bubble check, Buddy check, BO check

Best Practices

  1. Ideal Dive Profile:
    • Square or degressive profile with maximum depth at the beginning of the dive
    • Avoid yo-yo and staircase profiles
  2. Oxygen Flushing:
    • Practice at the end of the dive to:
      • Verify cell linearity/health
      • Reduce solenoid workload
      • Optimize decompression
      • Facilitate buoyancy
  3. Cell Maintenance:
    • Regular calibration to convert mV to bar
    • Never change all cells at the same time
    • Keep old cells as backup
    • Separate electronics (minimum 2, ideally 3)
    •  
  4. Using the Shearwater Petrel:
    • Understand the interface and menu navigation
    • Know how to interpret information screens (mV, PpO₂, tissues, etc.)
    • Monitor color codes (white: normal, yellow: caution, flashing red: life-threatening danger)

General Philosophy

  • Prevention rather than reaction: develop good verification habits
  • System redundancy: multiply information sources for safety
  • Manual regulation preferred: don't rely solely on automation
  • Constant vigilance: active monitoring of PpO₂, buddy, and environment
